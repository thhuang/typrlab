One color model.
Ten themes. Zero drift.

Every typr theme is generated from 8 signature hex colors, converted into 23 perceptually-uniform OKLCH tokens — surfaces, text, accent, feedback and effects. Components only ever reference the tokens, so swapping a theme retones the entire product with guaranteed contrast. Switch the theme above and watch every swatch and the live interface below update in lockstep.

Surfaces & structure

The elevation stack. bg is the page; panel / panel-2 lift cards and controls; the line pair draws borders and dividers; key-edge is the dark underside of a keycap.

Text & reading

Three reading weights. text is primary copy, muted is secondary/labels, and untyped is the dimmed-but-legible color for characters you haven't typed yet — brighter than muted on purpose.

Accent system

A single hue does all the lifting, in five calibrated strengths. on-accent is the contrast-safe text color the generator picks (dark text on light accents, near-white otherwise) so accent buttons stay readable in every theme.

Feedback & the confidence ramp

hit and miss mark correct and incorrect keystrokes. The same two colors anchor the confidence ramp — per-key mastery is mixed perceptually in OKLCH between slow-key-color (miss) and fast-key-color (hit), so the heatmap always matches the active theme.

Effects & geometry

Atmosphere tokens shared across themes. Two ambient glow washes sit behind the page, a film grain adds texture (lighter on light themes), and a single radius keeps every corner consistent.

The ten themes

Six dark (focus / low-light) and four light (daytime / reading & writing). Defaults: One Dark (dark) · Paper (light) — the rest are opt-in. Click any card to make it active everywhere on this page.
